Over the past 20 years, many things have changed and evolved. Fashion and aesthetic tastes, of course, have seen some of the most significant changes! Painting and decorating experts who have been in the game for a long time will tell you that what people are looking for in interior design in 2021 is, evidently, very different from 2001. Our last blog post here is a direct link to new colour trends for 2021 and how more adventurous painting and decorating styles have become.

Lots of factors affect the way we style our properties. Home decor trends are, ultimately, impacted by what’s happening in the world around us, with regard to art, fashion and culture. In the modern age, we are perhaps freer than ever before to explore a wild array of different looks and styles.

This is a big difference from times gone by when decor through the decades would revolve around having to keep on-trend. While there are always going to be trends and styles that people want to follow, there’s no longer this need to fit in!

With that in mind, let’s take a look at some of the decorating trends of the past 20 years – some of which you’re likely to remember!

Turn of The Century Trends

Domino

The 00s saw some interesting movements towards interior design based around technology. While we’d seen interior styles based around big TVs and Hi-Fis in the decades past, this was no longer a major point of contention. Our TVs were now slimmer, and people were at liberty to start setting up integrated speakers, and more besides.

The 2000s was also a decade for re-working old items and furniture into a new standard. ‘Vintage’ became a big draw towards the latter half of the decade, meaning that many homes adopted distressed or intentionally retro pieces to make their rooms fit in. It was also a big period for DIY, thanks to shifts in TV programming, meaning many people were keen to try and follow what worked on the telly.

Thankfully, the 00s saw an end to annoying puffed-up wallpaper and Artex style ceilings – while they are still around to some degree nowadays, they rather fell off at the end of the 90s.

What’s more, we really did love continental style back in the 00s – if you had a Tuscan themed bathroom, you already know what we mean!

The 10s: A Return to Happy Shabbiness?

Anthropologie

Anne Sage

By the 10s, many homeowners decided to stray from the polished look and instead swung towards ‘shabby chic’. That means many of us took on barn doors and worn wooden fittings in an attempt to make things look more ‘authentic’. The 10s, though we are barely out of them, was a decade all about focusing on natural, rather than synthetic, looks.

That went for kitchen spaces, too. There were huge changes in the kitchens of the 00s compared to the kitchens of the 10s. Instead of the whitewashed cabinets we saw pretty much everywhere at the turn of the millennium, we now saw people start to deconstruct their cupboards, opening up shelving to make things more accessible.

The entertainment station look in the lounge depleted even more, with the rise of smart technology and with home controls in the shape of built-in hubs. Tech devices slid into the background a little, meaning there was now much more emphasis on furnishings and colours.

Essentially, it became very cool in the 10s to get back to the meat of the matter – the actual look of your home, rather than the extra bits and bobs you might fill it with. Striking colours such as purple and pink may have seemed a bit gauche at the turn of 2010, meaning now really was the time to start looking at milder tones. However, if you are interested in the luxurious tones of purple, this read is for you!

Where Do We Go From Here?

Italian Bark

The 20s are here, and with them, there’s a whole raft of really interesting design trends opening up. That’s mainly thanks to the fact that social media now has a huge effect on our tastes more so than ever before – meaning we’re growing more eclectic than ever before.

Now’s the time to really show off your tastes and personality without guilt. Not sure how to do that? Get in touch with The Good Painter today and find out more about freeing yourself from trend constraints!

It’s safe to say that the exterior look of your home or building is very important. First impressions count! However, many of us do tend to focus more on what is going on inside of our homes or workplaces. Have you ever considered exterior painting and decorating before?

As lovely as your office may be inside, if the exterior is unmaintained, untouched, and ignored, it’s hardly going to inspire people to come inside! It’s not unheard of for people to hire an interior design team to spruce up the outside, too.

More and more people are considering decorating their exterior facades. Of course, designing your exterior the right way will require time, energy, and expertise! Thankfully, The Good Painter has a few ideas to help you get started – before you go ahead and splash too much exterior paint about!

Consider your materials

Sherwin Williams

Little Greene

The first thing that you will want to consider before starting any exterior painting and decorating job is getting suitable materials. No matter where you live, the weather will always affect the exterior of your home and your workplace. You need to make sure that the materials that you are planning on using outside are capable of withstanding the different circumstances that it will face.

It makes sense to look for specialist paint for outdoor surfaces and rough material. For example, you can invest in paint for use on metal as well as wooden posts and frames. Do also consider paint that’s going to withstand threats such a mould growth and UV.

If you’re really unsure about the paint and the tools you’ll need for exterior painting jobs, it pays to speak to London design professionals. A bit more on that a little further down! Outdoor spaces can look fantastic with a little TLC.

Plan ahead with common sense

As is the case when it comes to interior design, when you are planning on undergoing an exterior makeover for your home or workplace, then you need to be prepared for how long the process is going to take.

Your exterior painting will most likely not look perfect overnight, and in order for things to last for as long as possible outside, some groundwork will be necessary. Treating the outdoor materials before painting them, for example, is a must – and this will add to the overall time and effort taken.

Exterior paint jobs do require different preparation and materials from job to job. That is why interior design companies who also specialise in exterior painting and decorating, are very useful in these circumstances.

Consider colour trends!

Charleston Blonde

Jan Baldwin

If you are completely redoing a workplace’s exterior walls, then keeping up with the latest colour trends is an excellent idea! Making sure that your exterior follows current tastes will give your business a modern and sophisticated look. A top London interior design and build design studio will help point you in the right direction.

If your place of work is relatively new, then setting the style standards early on will help bolster your business’ image. It makes sense to get on-trend as soon as possible, and the outside of your building is the best place for this. Any design and build studio experts will agree with us!

If, however, your business has been around for a while, then you will already know that revamping the look of a building from the exterior will make it more likely to catch the eyes of passers-by. Even the most famous companies choose to recreate their looks and change their colour schemes from time to time.

Some of the top colour trends for 2021 are pale greys, blues and greens, as well as darker colours such as bronze, black, navy blue and earthy green. Why not try a mix while keeping on-brand? Ask for help from interior London experts if you’re unsure.

Our Team

When it comes to exterior painting and decorating, it is far better to hire professionals than to take it all on yourself. Keeping up with all of the latest exterior trends is one thing, but knowing what materials to use, what processes need to be completed to ensure the longevity of your paint job is completely different!

Hiring professional interior design experts means taking time, effort and expense off your shoulders. That means more time for you to actually run your business, and less time worrying about how it all looks.
