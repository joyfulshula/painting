Whenever the summer rolls around, we can all expect the colours around us to change. Things get bright, bold, and start to unwind. It’s a season for relaxing and for appreciating what we have. Therefore, it stands to reason that you should want to reflect this in your painting and decorating, too!

But where do you start when it comes to home interior design and painting in summer? What are some of the tones and shades you are most likely to come across when the weather gets better, and the trends seem to shift? We normally think of blues and yellows and oranges – but what do these typically reflect?

As a professional painting and decorating company, we are here to make sure you take away some top tips on how to get your home or commercial space looking great in summer 2021. It’s all about setting a positive mood and focusing on renewal. Here’s more about the upcoming colour forecast and the trends we are encouraged to use for this season!

Comforting Colours Make You Feel Grounded

Little Greene Paint Company

Jatana Tiles

Summer is all about rest and relaxation. Yes – daily life goes on – but you’re always going to need somewhere calm and inspiring to return to, especially this year. Home trends for summer 2021 really bring you back down to Earth – quite literally! Consider beiges, browns, terracottas and off-whites, for example.

These shades should help to ground you at the end of a long day. A great example is available right now through the Dulux catalogue. Skip To is a lovely, deep blend that really puts across a sense of security, calm, and contentment.

These brown and off-white blends are great summer choices for the simple fact that they take you back to nature. If you don’t have any natural touches in your interior design, a really quick shortcut is to choose comforting browns and beiges.

However, it’s also an excellent idea to consider embellishing your home with natural furnishings too. You should also think carefully about the shapes in your home – go for ergonomics and softness, from shape to texture and back again. Painting and decorating services can help you finalise that look!

Clean Shades of Blue = Instant Restoration

Crown Paints

Magali Saberian

Summer 2021 is, again, all about retreating and reviving – it’s a great season to build on your accomplishments. Blue actually remains one of the most Insta-friendly colours ever, and there’s a good reason for that.

Blue is soft, summery and approachable, and palettes based in its most popular shades and hues are often surprisingly simple, too. You can pair a rich, hearty blue with a simple or warm white for that added sophistication. Neutral greys, too, can help to add to a reviving colour scheme with blue at heart.

However, you don’t have to go all-blue unless you want to. Several shades of colour work wonderfully with warm accents and touches, meaning you don’t have to rely on it as a standalone choice. In fact, contrasting cool blues with warm touches is an excellent idea if you want to accentuate your interior design. Blue can be contemporary or classic, but the best part is, it’s always optimistic – the ideal look for summer.

Encouraging Boldness, Embracing Colour

West Elm

Soho Home

Summer is a time when we all look forward to a change in the scenery and a change in pace. It’s when things get bright and bold and when we start to look forward to drabness fade away. Some of the best home trends right now revolve around boldness through retro influence.

Summer colours for 2021 tend to do better in pairs. It’s all about celebrating diversity and create upbeat pairings that add quirk and personality to your interiors. For example, you may consider using reds with greens and with touches of blue. Red and green aren’t just a pairing people associate with Christmas – it’s a summer look too, when used in small ounces and in moderation.

For more inspiration, we highly recommend the ideal palette available in the Dulux range right here.

Our favourite paint colours for children’s bedroom

Ready to renovate your children’s bedroom or want to create a bedroom when starting a family? The Good Painter have rounded up 7 of our favourite paint colours for a child-friendly space to encourage positivity, calmness and optimism! Inspire and experiment with these colour selections fit for a boy or girls room in your London home. Our painters and decorators guarantee you’ll find the perfect fit to start your painting project today! If you are looking for residential painters and decorators to help assist with your refurbishment and painting project, make sure to visit our services and enquire. Without further ado, let us explore the range of paint colours to create an impeccable bedroom to make your child ecstatic!

1.Dayroom Yellow – Farrow & Ball

Farrow & Ball

Yellow is the favourite neutral-colour that works beautifully in any room, especially as it is a lively and bright shade to represent the sunshine and sun glow. Dayroom Yellow by Farrow & Ball is the ideal shade to reflect natural sunlight and all-in-all portray a beaming and joyful display for your child. This paint colour will work wonderfully as a statement wall or even on painted furniture to change things up a bit. Make sure to check out our blog post on how to paint your wooden furniture perfectly.

2. Hidey Hole – Little Greene

Little Greene Paint Company

Who doesn’t want to be surrounded in an adventurous and natural scene representing the exterior? Hidey Hole is the peaceful, light green which fits wonderfully in your space and allows your inspiration to flourish! Produce a fantastic and imaginative room for your children with green and encourage a healthy atmosphere in your home!

3. Lavender – Edward Bulmer

Edward Bulmer

This striking smokey mauve from Edward Bulmer creates a pretty and charming bedroom fit for a girl, especially in a monochromatic scheme. Our painters and decorators recommend to pair this paint colour and adding a statement wall with the Aesops Fables wallpaper by Sanderson. Create a magical and dreamy environment with this timeless, dusky shade.

4. First Dawn – Dulux UK

Dulux UK

One of our favourite colours due to its versatility, portrayal and meaning. This gorgeous sky-like blue from Dulux is called First Dawn and is the perfect shade and paint colour for a children’s bedroom. Build an environment which is relaxing, tranquil and gentle for your little one, while splendidly working with the lighting. First Dawn is definitely one of the picks for an enchanting bedroom.

5. Fruit Shake – Benjamin Moore

Benjamin Moore

Fruit Shake is that extraordinary shade which illuminates the space with its soft, gentle femininity energy. Create a pleasantly pink and positive environment with this creamy shade, not too bright and not overly saturated, just right! Pink is a cheerful and charming colour full of compassion. Are you looking to find more positive colours? We have a blog post with our top positive shades, so make sure to stay content!

6. Verdigris Green – Farrow & Ball

Farrow & Ball

For anyone looking for a brighter and more daring paint colour, our painters and decorators haven’t forgotten about you. Verdigris Green is from Farrow & Balls Colour by Nature palette and creates a room which is lively and tropical. This rich blend will fit a neutral colour scheme, so it is not too overbearing for your child. If you are looking to experiment and construct the ideal colour scheme, visit Color Designer and get inspired.

7. Ice V – Paint & Paper Library

Paint & Paper Library

Finally, the Ice V from Paint & Paper Library will conclude our favourite paint colours list. This icy blue works beautifully on your children’s bedroom walls, paired with a darker shade for woodwork and trims. Create a versatile and fun space with this paint colour; we love the simplicity and how it is the perfect base for endless inspiration and ideas!
