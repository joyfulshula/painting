How can office painting affect your mood and the work you deliver?

It’s long-established that colours can affect our wellbeing in a big way.  Our relationships with different shades and hues trace back centuries!  There are different colours which inspire different feelings and which can drive us to our goals.  When it comes to painting and decorating, we often find we are drawn to calming tones and dynamic shades, and these can depend on what we use our rooms for.

Different colours could help in office painting because of their effects on our state of mind and wellbeing.  For example, red could help to stimulate proactivity, and shades of green can bring a sense of calm.  Therefore, it can be crucial to choose paint colours carefully for the workplace!  Let’s explore this science in a little more detail with our painters and decorators’ best selections for office paint colours.

Red

Red is an urgent colour!  It inspires proactivity and encourages us to be alert and to take action.  Studies show that it is a driving colour for creative spaces because it encourages us to press forward with our work.  Red can help wonderfully when physical activity is a must, and it is perfect for fast-moving offices.  Need to really spice up a productive workplace?  Red is a fantastic colour to start with.

Blue

On the opposite end of the scale, blue is a very calming colour.  It inspires precision and clear thinking.  When painting and decorating an accountancy office or a management space, blue is a colour you will likely rely on.  Unlike red, shades of blue are less suited to physical jobs and more to work where you use a lot of brain power.  It’s also very easy to pair with neutral shades.  We used blue to highlight brand aesthetics and boost concentration for the Betbull team, painting and decorating their large office at High Holborn.

Yellow is much like red in that it is a stimulating colour which inspires lots of energy.  For a creative space, yellow is perfect because it helps to enhance mood.  It is also wonderfully versatile, which is an asset to office painting where a lot of neutral colours are employed.  Marketing firms and design studios, for example, will benefit from yellow paint.

Purple

Purple is a striking, dynamic colour.  A painter and decorator will recommend purple as an accent to other shades.  However, it is perfect for stimulating productivity and creative thought.  Darker shades help to promote passion and drive.  We used purple extensively in our recent creative studio project, painting and decorating a video game producer’s studio in Vauxhall, London.

Ready to renovate your space this season and want to start painting? To complete a top-quality painting job, our painters and decorators share some insight on what should and shouldn’t be done when painting and decorating. Grabbing some paint and buying brushes is all you need. Wrong. To produce a professional and clean job, we must have the correct preparation and equipment. Here are some top painting mistakes from The Good Painter that should be avoided to perform an exceptional job and renovate your residential or commercial space flawlessly.

Not considering paint samples in the room

It’s easy to find paint which you are fond of and want to purchase it for your room immediately. The mistake with this is, we’re not entirely aware of how it might turn out in the room. Many factors are considered, such as lighting. The colour you fell in love within that bright light room might not be a replica when painted in a room with natural lighting or not much light. Our expert team advise to find many samples and apply on the wall of your room to see what actually stands out to you and what would fit well in the space.

Not preparing the walls or space

Prepping is critical when wanting a smooth painting and decorating job and an uncomplicated journey. Covering floors, furniture and removing pieces will help you in the long run and can avoid headaches if the paint was to ruin your special belongings. Wrapping doorknobs and taping switch covers are just examples of easy steps to add, so everything looks clean and professional in the end. Also, cleaning the walls is something which is often forgotten but necessary to have a smooth finish. It’s not pleasant having a wall with dirt, scratches or hair particles stuck in the wall. Our painters and decorators believe cleaning your walls simply with a rag and warm water can make a significant difference to the quality of your job.

Using the right paint and equipment

You finally found a shade you like and want to go for it, however when you go to the store you’re faced with many types of paints and formulas which may be entirely new for you. You may also have to consider the surface you are working with. Has it been treated with an oil-based formula? Applying latex paint on top of it may cause the paint to peel. The Good Painter are experts regarding what the best solution is, therefore, it’s worth considering finding a painting and decorating service who can assist you. Our painters and decorators share their reasons on why using a professional service will be beneficial and cost-effective

Skipping primer when painting

Priming your walls provides a clean and quality surface to accentuate the paint and facilitate the adhesion. When painting and decorating, the first layer to apply is primer as it helps bring out the colour you desire and conceals blemishes. It is critical as it will be time and cost-effective in the long run, avoiding painting coats and coats on the wall.

Painting in low light

This may seem like an obvious point, but make sure you’re fully prepared when painting. Painting and decorating in dim or low light may seem fine at the time, but when introducing any artificial lighting or bright lights, all the mistakes, missing spots or stroke marks will be seen and fully visible. Enjoy the experience. Make sure the room is well lit with natural light, you have your favourite music on and have fun with it! The best time to schedule is early morning, where it’s the brightest part of the day where a lot of natural light will come in.

Painting: Is there a difference between exterior and interior paint?

This is probably a question many of us have asked ourselves when deciding on what paint to buy when painting. Is it necessary to buy different paints? Is there actually a difference, and if so, what is it?

Our painters and decorators do both: interior and exterior painting, so we know the differences. Here is a list…

The purpose for painting

The difference may seem an obvious one but is important to explain. Exterior paint has a completely different purpose than the paint used inside. The exterior paint is supposed to resist all sorts of exposures like weather and humidity. There can be quick temperature changes that will make the walls expand and contract, to which the paint needs to adjust to, as otherwise it will crack. Exterior paint is also made to dry as quickly as possible and different environments to allow exterior wall painting to be done, not only when the weather is good. However, this also means that to allow of this, the compounds in the exterior paint will have to be different to the ones in the interior paint.

The purpose of interior paint on the other hand, is for aesthetic reasons but also for daily use and cleaning.

Does that mean it is possible to use interior paint for exterior painting?

It is possible, however not recommended. If you love the colour you have used for your interior, it is mostly likely that you can get that same colour at a store especially made for exterior painting. If you can’t find it, our painters and decorators offer colour consultation services and are trained in the latest painting techniques, which means they mix and blend colours until they find the one you are looking for.

Reasons to why interior paint is not as useful is because it simply isn’t made to resist all those exposures. Interior paint is usually much thinner, which means it will take many coats to even apply a relatively thin layer of it on the outside. What may be enough to cover patches in the living room, may not look as good on the outside of your home. Lastly, the problem with this paint is that is doesn’t dry in cold or moist weathers, which means that it will get dirty and unaesthetic very quickly.

Does that mean it makes sense to use exterior paint for interior painting?

If exterior paint is resistant to humidity, it seems to make sense to paint your bathroom with it, but the truth is you shouldn’t.  Not only that it is not meant for that purpose and will simply not look good, but it is also quite health damaging. Exterior paint that is supposed to last through weather exposures and a good amount of years has certain compounds that create fumes. While being meant to use outside exposed to the air, exterior paint in the interior exposes fumes due to poor ventilation and trigger respiratory problems as asthma, cause headaches and even nausea. Apart from that, exterior paint is not made to be open to daily use and is unprotected against chemicals like stain removers or detergents.

So, if you are facing mould problems due to humidity, which is often the case in London, try airing more if weather allows it. If not, it is always possible to use a humidity absorber device. They are small and usually cheap and absorb the humidity out of the air to prevent mould spreading.

What to do if exterior paint was used for interior painting

If you read this post, recently after having painted your interior walls with exterior painting, don’t worry! You can always paint over the other paint. However, firstly you have to prepare the walls, by removing the previous layer of exterior paint on your walls, so that the fumes will not keep passing through the freshly used interior paint.

If you are unsure of how to do this or you are worried of doing something wrong, our painters and decorators are available as soon as the next day!

Final notes on exterior and interior painting…

Whether doing interior or exterior painting, the colours and paints you chose is what you will live with for years. On top of that, the painting process itself and the preparation process can be quite hasslesome, especially if it needs to be redone. It is always best to ask for advice first and our painters and decorators are always there for consultation. Our customer service responds to inquiries within two hours and are years of experience have prepared us to answer almost any question regarding painting, whether exterior or interior and are ready to help with the process.

What is the Scandi trend and where did it come from?

If you are unsure of what the words fika and hygge mean, they most probably sound familiar to you. These words come from Scandinavia, which apart from being considered the happiest countries in the world by the UN World Happiness Report, are also the creators of many trends including the Scandi look for interior design.

The painting and decorating trend focuses on simplicity and minimalism rather than sophistication and started as a movement in the 1950s in Scandinavia (Norway, Denmark and Sweden). It is mainly about the use of earthy colours and embracing nature in one’s home through its materials. Being an answer to those long winter days and an attempt to bring cosiness into their homes, the Scandi look successfully spread and became increasingly popular.

How a painter and decorator can transform your London home in 3 days

Painting and decorating anything can seem like a massive project, but it doesn’t have to be. Most of us know when it’s time to refurbish, but the fear of the process and the mess can be overwhelming. No exception were most of our clients whether they needed to refurbish a family house or a small apartment. For most of the process, you often don’t even have to be there. Ever wondered what happens when you’re not there and how your home actually gets transformed? Here is everything that can happen in only 3 days’ time…

These 3 days are based on the refurbishing of an average 2 Bedroom home. We will now go into the details of the process, so even if you are not there, you are well aware of what is going on.

Preparation for painting and decorating
Paint colour is agreed in advance

Before starting painting and decorating, it is firstly decided on what colour will be used. Our team of painters and decorators offer Colour Consultation services and are professionally trained in the latest painting techniques. Through colour consultation we help you decide what colour you want to use, using paint samples to check a colour at different exposure of light and room orientation.

This is important because paint samples on the wall give a more accurate impression of what the colour will actually look like. If you simply need a freshening up of the walls by painting but you can’t remember what paint colour it is, don’t worry. Our painters and decorators can mix and blend until they find the perfect match.

If you are completely unsure about what colour scheme you want, our painters and decorators will help you find a palette that will go with your style and personality. There is a wide range of colours and finishes to choose from.
Once agreed, the painting and decorating is ready to begin.

How to prepare for the painting

For this, before the painting and decorating begins, you can start preparing the rooms or house that you are going to have renovated.

Firstly, arrange access so that nothing is in the way. Furniture that is heavy or big will later on be moved by painters and decorators as part of the process. What you can do, is provide for a clean, decluttered working area and make sure that there will be access available for when the painting begins

Painting and decorating begins…
The team will early to have an early start

Our team of painters and decorators takes timeframes very seriously. We always operate within timeframes, and for that we like starting as early as possible. This is necessary because before the painting and decorating begins, there is still some preparation left to do. We believe that good preparation is the first step to satisfying and long-lasting results, which is why we put care and time into it.

Furniture will be moved and protected

We put value into taking care of your belongings. That is why any furniture, even if large will be moved by our painters and decorators into the middle of the room. Any furniture or of your belongings will be covered until clean dust sheets so that they are protected from any dirt or paint.

Preparing the walls

Before the painting and decorating begins, the walls have to be prepared. Firstly, the best supplies are bought. Then any defects in the walls need to be taken of. Some flaws like unsmooth walls or woodwork are taken away using careful sanding, to make the surfaces perfectly smooth for easy application and long-lasting results. Any cracks and holes will be filled with a substance and prepared before the painting. Before paint is applied, it is usual for a tinted primer to be used to correct any discolorations due to the filled in cracks. The colour of the substance used to unify the walls can make the paint over it have another colour than the rest of the wall. That is why tinted primer is used, so that when the walls are painted the colour is even across the wall. Another benefit of primer it will prep the walls, make the paint last longer and allow less coats of paint needed for full opacity.

Painting

This is the first moment of change your home is going to live through in this process. Depending on the colour choices you have decided on previously, painters will use different techniques to start painting the walls. It is normal for the trims to be taped to prevent any uneven application and paint in places it is not supposed to be. The painters will roll and brush until the perfect colour is achieved. If it is a feature wall you are aiming for, painters will usually gently go over the ridges in the walls to make sure that when they paint, it will remain within that one wall only and keep the rest clean. Also, our painters will always keep you up-to-date throughout the process, to make sure that everything is going the way you want it to.

Cleaning up

After all the painting and decorating has been done, for the perfect finish, our painters and decorators will leave your home sparkling. We will make sure that there are no paint splashes or stains left anywhere they aren’t supposed to. The floors and any other areas will be left clean as well. Dust sheets will be removed from the furniture and taken care of, whilst the furniture will be put back into place. Lastly, after having cleaned up everything from any residues, paint or dust, the painters will ask you to look over their work and whether you are satisfied with it. This is especially important to us, as we always aim for a 100% customer satisfaction.

Best Colour Schemes for Living Room

Living rooms are usually found to be one of the biggest rooms. It is also the room where families gather to enjoy moments together or where friends come over for endless game nights. However, it is also a place that should allow to relax from a long day and enjoy some peaceful alone time. Painting and decorating the living room can therefore be challenging, but here are some colours selected by our professional painters and decorators to help you out:

Image Source: Pinterest 

Image Source: Pinterest 

Blue

Blue is a very soothing colour that comes with many undertones and has a relaxing and calming effect on the mind, which makes it perfect for painting the walls in the living room.

When painting and decorating with blue, it also improves communication and concentration, which makes it the perfect colour for socialising in the living room.

Another quality of blue is that no matter which tone you use, most often it will make the room look more spacious. If you decide for painting with lighter blues, you can try this in rooms with a lot of natural light to make the most of the reflected light. When decorating with light blue walls, you could try using darker colours or elements that fit in the colour scheme or simply are darker tones of blue. For example, you could try decorating with a dark blue or yellow sofa. Depending on the tone of blue, decorating can be quite easy compared to other colours, because it can act a beautiful colour combination with most colours. You could use colours like orange, white, green, pink, black and even yellow. The colour mix of white, blue and yellow can give a fresh look to the room. Neutrals

Boring to some, neutrals remains a classic for many reasons. Using a neutral colour schemes allows to head off into any direction you want when it comes to decorating. For sophistication, you can always add grey through decorations or use neutral colours with grey tones. However, if you change your mind about the style you want to go for and want to create a more joyful atmosphere for your guests, you can always add in pops of colour. You could try and add colourful pillows, fluffy blankets or rugs to create the to use the same colour scheme as the wall, throughout the room for a unified look, just use different textures and materials when decorating. This will give the room coherence, rather than monotony and contribute to a homely feeling.

Grey

Grey is another colour that belongs to the family of classics and is very versatile. Being a timeless colour, you can use grey to achieve almost any look. To create a sense of sophistication you can try painting the walls a dark grey and allow the ceiling to stay a lighter colour. This will make the room look more spacious and unusual.

If you want to experiment new techniques of decorating, you could paint one wall of the living room in grey and create your own feature wall. You could put a grey sofa or furniture item in front of it to break the room into geometric spaces for even more statement and make use of the effect of colour blocking. This can be original and leave your guests with an impression of your painting and decorating.

For people who don’t want to risk the living room looking too dark with grey walls, our painters and decorators recommend using lighter colours like creamy whites and add some grey to it.

When it comes to painting and decorating, grey acts similar to neutrals and is a great opportunity to try out any pops of colour you wanted to experiment with, without overwhelming the room. Try lavenders and pinks for a more serene atmosphere or rusty oranges, yellow or reds for brighter colours when decorating. The greys will balance out the colours and make the room look authentic. White offers a different range of undertones which allows it to be a great colour to use for most rooms. Best Colour Schemes for painting and decorating your Kitchen

To most people, the kitchen is the favourite part of the house. It’s the room where families get together, cook and spend time together. The best meals and the most natural conversations all happen in the kitchen. That is why you want to put the love of painting and decorating your kitchen to feel comfortable in it. As so much time is spent in the kitchen, it is important to make the colour choice a right one! Here are some possible colour schemes our painters and decorators have compiled for you…

Shades of Green, Yellow and/or Pink

Green is colour that will bring warmth and nature into your own home. Of course, there are many different shades to choose from. You can give your kitchen a fresh modern look by painting in colours like lime, mint, blue-greens or even peacock. It is said to be a rule to use colours for decorating that are opposite each other on the colour wheel, so the opposite to green would be pink. Depending on the shade, pink and green go very well together. You can try creating a contrast by going darker on the green, the lighter you go on the pink or other way around. You can also add pops of yellow through decoration like lamps or use light yellow instead of pink. When choosing a colour scheme, you should usually stick to two to three colours. So if your decorations or furniture are made of wood or white, you probably only want to choose two other colours only.

The combination of these two classic colours leads to a beautiful maritime kitchen. Try using darker blues to build on the contrast with the whites. This colour mix manages to maintain a neutrality while at the same time not being too overwhelming with colour. You can either integrate these colours through painting the walls or through decorating like for example, tiles. Use warm toned blues to balance out the whites, that are usually cool toned. This will some stability and style to the room. If you are painting your walls blue and white, wooden decorations will warm up the room and make it cosy.

You can also add pops of colour, but make sure that they complement the blue you have chosen.
